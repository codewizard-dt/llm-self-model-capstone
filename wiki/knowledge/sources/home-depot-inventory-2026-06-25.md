# Home Depot Supplemental Build Inventory, 2026-06-25

This user-provided source records the confirmed Home Depot hardware now available for the scoop/flywheel build. It materially improves the previous inventory note: there are now **two 12 in galvanized strap plates, one 8 in galvanized strap plate, four 3/8 in-long nylon spacers, four 1/2 in-long nylon spacers, plenty of zip ties and Velcro straps, and very soft foam balls**.

## Build Impact

The scoop should use the 8 in strap as the adapter spine when possible, with zip ties or Velcro providing two separated clamp stations around the spoon or dustpan. The flywheel frame can be represented as two parallel 12 in strap side plates separated by nylon spacers, with the flywheel shaft running perpendicular through the side plates.

The foam balls should be treated as uncertain flywheel objects. Because they compress nearly flat, they may absorb energy rather than rebound through the flywheel. They are still useful for scoop pickup testing and very low-risk feed testing, but the flywheel plan should keep the racquetball or a less-collapsible foam ball as the better launch candidate.
