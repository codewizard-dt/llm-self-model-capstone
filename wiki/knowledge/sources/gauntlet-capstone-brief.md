# Gauntlet Capstone Kickoff Brief 2026

The official brief for the 2026 Gauntlet capstone. **Three pages**, covering the evaluation philosophy, schedule, project directions, and deliverables. derives_from::[[Gauntlet]]

## Philosophy

**The capstone is not measured by polish — it is measured by the ambition of what you attempted and how much of it got working.** Pick a problem that is technically hard. This is the proof of what you can do with AI as a core primitive, not a side project.

## Three Project Directions

**A — Combine ML with LLM applications.** Pair classical machine learning models with LLM-driven systems so each does what it's best at.

**B — A philosophically different take on agents.** Rethink how agents are leveraged. Example: could a company survive if a team worked through one shared agent that did most of the work — and the team simply verified it?

**C — One problem, many approaches.** Take a simple problem and attack it from several angles — solving it multiple ways and comparing what actually wins.

## Deliverables

**Deliverable 01 — Project Planning Document.** Due Wed Jun 17 at 11:59PM. Hard deadline. One submission per team. Contents: the problem, the technical approach, scope, and who owns what.

**Deliverable 02 — Live Presentation.** Mon Jun 29, 10 minutes, live, in front of the room. Demo what you built and what you learned.

## Notes

- Team size: 3–4 challengers
- "Build something that proves what you can do. Start now."
- The brief does **not** specify a minimum tech stack, language, or domain — all three directions are framed as patterns, not prescriptions
