# Flywheel Recut Plan for Two VEX 5x15 Steel Plates

This source records the inventory-aware recut decision for the flywheel assembly. With two VEX 5x15 Steel Plates available and no spare U-channel or C-channel, the VEX plates should become the precision flywheel cassette material while Home Depot straps remain secondary brace, chute, and backplate stock.

Here `5x15`, `5x10`, `5x8`, `5x7`, and `5x5` are VEX hole-grid counts, not inch dimensions. With 0.5 in pitch, nominal grid spans are 2.5 in x 7.5 in for `5x15`, 2.5 in x 5.0 in for `5x10`, and 2.5 in x 2.5 in for `5x5`.

The preferred cut is a **5x8 + 5x7 split on each VEX plate**. The two 5x8 pieces become matched side plates for the flywheel shaft bearings; the two 5x7 pieces become the adjustable backplate, chute wall, top bridge, lower rail, or motor brace. This gives the best balance of compact side plates and still-useful VEX-grid leftovers.

The second-best cut is a **5x10 + 5x5 hole-grid split on each VEX plate**. The two 5x10 pieces, nominally 2.5 in x 5.0 in by hole-count times pitch, become longer side plates with more fore-aft adjustment room; the two 5x5 pieces, nominally 2.5 in x 2.5 in, become gussets or motor brackets. This is better if the fixed-arm mount or ball path remains uncertain, but it leaves less VEX-grid material for a long backplate.

Avoid lengthwise strip cuts for bearing side plates, and avoid splitting the plates into many small pieces before the first flywheel cassette is proven. Full-width cross-cuts between hole rows preserve bearing alignment and are easier to cut cleanly with hand snips.
