# Capstone Proposal / Planning Document — Required Structure

A concise rubric defining the seven sections every relates_to::[[Gauntlet]] capstone proposal and Project Planning Document (Deliverable 01) must contain. **Ambition is the scoring criterion, not polish.** Fifteen proposals are selected; winning proposers then recruit teams of 3–4 challengers, so the proposal also functions as a team pitch.

## The Seven Required Sections

1. **The problem** — what you're solving and why it's technically hard. This is the primary scoring axis.
2. **Project direction** — which of the three Gauntlet directions it fits: (A) ML + LLM, (B) a philosophically different agent design, or (C) one problem attacked many ways.
3. **Technical approach** — the core architecture and how AI functions as a primitive, not a bolt-on.
4. **Scope** — what "working" looks like in two weeks; the ambitious version vs. the minimum shippable cut.
5. **Why it's compelling** — the hook that makes others want to join your team.
6. **Hard parts / risks** — the technical bet you're making and where it could break.
7. **What you'd demo** — a basic sketch of the live 10-minute showcase on Jun 29.
