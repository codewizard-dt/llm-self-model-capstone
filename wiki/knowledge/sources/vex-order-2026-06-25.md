# VEX Order and Known Build Inventory, 2026-06-25

This user-provided source records the parts ordered for the next VEX build pass and the current known inventory constraints. The key operational change is that the next flywheel/scoop plan **cannot assume spare U-channel or C-channel**; it must use ordered 5x15 steel plates, extra spacers, and the available non-VEX perforated steel pieces until a fuller inventory is confirmed.

## Known Inventory Constraints

- No spare U-channels.
- No spare C-channels.
- Extra spacers are available.
- Extra non-VEX perforated steel plates are available: approximately 1.5 in x 8 in and 1.5 in x 12 in, with scattered holes similar to galvanized mending plates.
- More inventory will be updated after the next physical count.

## Build Planning Impact

The known inventory changes the flywheel frame approach from a C-channel side-plate frame to a **plate-and-spacer sandwich frame**. The ordered 5x15 steel plates should be treated as the precision VEX-compatible side plates; non-VEX perforated plates are better suited to scoop adapter spines, braces, chute walls, or backplates unless their hole pattern is measured and confirmed.

## Home Depot Inventory Update

derived_from::[[home-depot-inventory-2026-06-25]] confirms the actual supplemental hardware: two 12 in Simpson Strong-Tie strap plates, one 8 in strap plate, four 3/8 in-long nylon spacers, four 1/2 in-long nylon spacers, plenty of zip ties and Velcro straps, and very soft foam balls. No more VEX parts will be ordered, but additional Home Depot spacers can be purchased if needed.

This makes the scoop clamp adapter stronger: use the 8 in strap as the spoon/dustpan spine. For flywheel prototyping, the two 12 in straps can act as side plates if their shaft/bearing holes are measured and aligned; the ordered VEX 5x15 plates remain preferable if available because their hole pattern is known.
