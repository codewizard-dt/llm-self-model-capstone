# Brian Scassellati

**Professor**, Yale University, Department of Computer Science. Director of the **Social Robotics Lab** (scazlab.yale.edu). One of the most cited researchers in social robotics and developmental robotics.

Scassellati is relates_to::[[justin-hart]]'s PhD advisor. He co-authored all of Hart's self-modeling work:
- "Robotic Self-Models Inspired by Human Development" (AAAI 2010 workshop)
- "Robotic Models of Self" (MIT Press, *Metareasoning*, 2011)
- "A Robotic Model of the Ecological Self" (IEEE-RAS HUMANOIDS 2011)
- "Mirror Perspective-Taking with a Humanoid Robot" (AAAI 2012)
- "Robotic Self-Modeling" (Imperial College Press, 2014)
- "Self-Awareness and Social Competencies" (AMD Newsletter, 2015)
- "Robot Self-Modeling" (International Journal of Humanoid Robotics, 2017)

The Scazzellati lab's research tradition is **developmental robotics** — robots that learn about themselves and the social world the way human infants do. The robot Nico, used in Hart's self-modeling work, was built at the Social Robotics Lab.

The capstone's [[llm-authored-self-model]] concept is a direct extension of the Scassellati lab's self-modeling lineage, updated with LLM authoring and adversarial critique.
