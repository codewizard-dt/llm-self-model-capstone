# Jim Fan

relates_to::[[NVIDIA GEAR Lab]]
relates_to::[[Agentic Physical Autoresearch]]

Linxi "Jim" Fan is **Director of AI & Distinguished Scientist at NVIDIA** and co-lead of Project GR00T (humanoid robotics) and the GEAR Lab (Generalist Embodied Agent Research). Stanford Ph.D., OpenAI's first intern. His research mission is building generally capable agents across physical worlds (robotics) and virtual worlds (games, simulation).

**Prior landmark work**: Voyager (first AI agent that plays Minecraft and bootstraps capabilities continuously), MineDojo (open-ended learning from 100K+ Minecraft YouTube videos), Eureka (GPT-4 writes reward functions for 5-finger pen-spinning), VIMA (multimodal foundation model for robot manipulation).

**ENPIRE**: Fan is an equal advisor on the ENPIRE project alongside Yuke Zhu (NVIDIA) and Guanya Shi (CMU). The project's thesis — that the missing abstraction for autonomous robotics research is a repeatable physical feedback loop — is a direct expression of his broader research agenda: shifting the bottleneck in robotics from algorithm design to closed physical feedback loops an agent can turn on its own.
