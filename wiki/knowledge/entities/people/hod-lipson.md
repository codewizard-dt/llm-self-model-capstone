# Hod Lipson

Roboticist (Columbia, formerly Cornell) whose work is the **foundational research lineage** for the physical-robot-factory idea. With Jordan Pollack, authored "Automatic Design and Manufacture of Robotic Lifeforms" (*Nature*, 2000) — the GOLEM project that evolved robot designs in simulation and then 3D-printed the winners — and "Self-Reproducing Machines" (*Nature*, 2005).

## Why He Recurs

The feasibility reports cite Lipson & Pollack as the canonical evidence that **morphology search must happen over a restricted design language** (they evolved robots from basic building blocks, not arbitrary CAD) — the basis of [[typed-assembly-grammar]]. His lineage runs forward to DERL, Text2Robot, and the Brodbeck "mother robot," all of which the reports use to argue that bounded autonomous robot design+build is no longer speculative.
