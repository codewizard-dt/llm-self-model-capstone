# Gauntlet

An intensive AI engineering program whose capstone brief is the primary driver of this project. **Gauntlet sets the evaluation criteria** that all capstone proposals must satisfy.

The capstone brief (uploaded as context in the derives_from::[[chatgpt-ai-powered-software-factory]] brainstorm) explicitly calls out ML+LLM hybrids, new agent paradigms, and multiple approaches to the same problem as desirable. It poses the question: "Could a company survive if a team worked through one shared agent and humans verified?" The brief is used as a reference point for scoring every proposed idea against technical ambition, demo-ability, and AI-nativeness.

Gauntlet expects a 3–4 person team to produce a working, live-demo-able system in approximately two weeks. Projects that look like "LLM wrappers" or CRUD apps are explicitly discouraged.

## Evaluation Philosophy (verbatim)

> "The capstone is not measured by polish — it's measured by the ambition of what you attempted and how much of it got working."

## Three Official Directions

- **A**: Combine ML with LLM applications — classical ML + LLM-driven systems, each doing what it's best at
- **B**: A philosophically different take on agents — e.g., one shared agent does most work; team verifies
- **C**: One problem, many approaches — attack from several angles and compare what wins

## Cohort 5 Masterclass

Gauntlet ran a Cohort 5 masterclass, "The Future of AI Product Engineering" ([[gauntlet-g5-masterclass-ai-product-engineering]]), arguing that AI commoditizes execution and that **product judgment** is the differentiator. It teaches the [[shape-ship-sync]] framework and the [[brainlift]] methodology. This trains the judgment layer that sits above the capstone's technical-ambition bar.

## Proposal / Planning Document Structure

Gauntlet selects 15 proposals from all submissions (due Mon Jun 15 at 6PM). Winners recruit teams of 3–4. The required planning document sections are documented in derives_from::[[capstone-proposal-structure]]: problem, direction, technical approach, scope, why compelling, hard parts/risks, and demo sketch. Scoring is on ambition, not polish.
