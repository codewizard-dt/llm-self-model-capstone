# NVIDIA GEAR Lab

relates_to::[[Agentic Physical Autoresearch]]
led_by::[[Jim Fan]]

The **Generalist Embodied Agent Research (GEAR) Lab** at NVIDIA, co-led by [[jim-fan|Jim Fan]] and Prof. Yuke Zhu. Mission: build foundation models for embodied agents in virtual and physical worlds. Research agenda covers LLMs for planning and reasoning, vision-language models, world models trained on Internet-scale data, and robotic models for locomotion and dexterous manipulation.

## Key Projects

- **Project GR00T** — humanoid robot foundation models (GR00T N1, N1.5, N1.7); co-led by Jim Fan
- **ENPIRE** — agentic robot policy self-improvement; four-module harness giving coding agents a physical feedback loop; 99% success on dexterous manipulation
- **Voyager, MineDojo, Eureka, VIMA** — earlier landmark work by Jim Fan (game/simulation agents, reward function generation)
- **DreamZero** — robot foundation model with zero-shot, open-world prompting for new verbs/nouns/environments

## ENPIRE Team

Lead authors: Wenli Xiao, Jia Xie, Tonghe Zhang, Haotian Lin. Affiliated: NVIDIA, Carnegie Mellon University (LeCAR Lab / Guanya Shi), UC Berkeley (Shankar Sastry, Ken Goldberg, Letian Fu, Haoru Xue).
