# LEGO Education Computer Science & AI Kit

The platform LEGO Education announced (12 January 2026) as the **successor to the retiring SPIKE Portfolio**. K-8 classroom kits for CS and AI curriculum, shipping from April 2026 (grades 6-8 / 45522 first; K-2 / 45520 ships September 2026), starting at **$339.95 per kit ($84.98/student, 4 students)**. The grades 6-8 kit contains **1 double motor, 1 single motor, 1 color sensor, 1 controller, 2 connection cards, and ~379 bricks**, programmed via a block-based Coding Canvas (Python rumored for April 2026, VS Code-based).

> **Not a viable substitute for this capstone.** Despite replacing [[lego-spike-prime]] in the FIRST LEGO League pipeline (from the 2026-2027 season), the CS & AI kit is a **classroom curriculum tool, not a robotics research platform**: fewer motors, a card-based program-selection model, and a constrained toolchain. It does not provide the finite-but-rich typed parts vocabulary the [[llm-authored-self-model]] requires. The project's Stage-2 migration target remains [[vex-v5]], not this kit.
