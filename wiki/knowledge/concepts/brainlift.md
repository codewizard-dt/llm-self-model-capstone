# BrainLift (DOK 1–4)

A structured methodology, taught in the Cohort 5 masterclass, for building **defensible, contrarian expertise** in any domain. The deck calls it "your unfair advantage." It is also the basis of a graded assignment.

## The Four Levels (Depth of Knowledge)

- **DOK 1 · Facts** — raw data points from credible, primary sources. The ground truth.
- **DOK 2 · Knowledge Tree** — organized summaries in your own words: Categories › Sub-categories › Sources › Facts › Summaries.
- **DOK 3 · Insights** — original connections between facts from different sources; your critical thinking.
- **DOK 4 · Spiky POVs** — strong, defensible, *contrarian* arguments forged from multiple insights. **"A Spiky POV is a well-reasoned, contrarian argument — not an opinion,"** and it "cannot be generated by AI alone."

## The Research Workflow That Feeds It

Five phases: **Initiation** (scope) → **Source Aggregation** (build the tree from primary sources) → **Synthesis** (draft DOK 1→4) → **Validation ★** (red-team your own work) → **Finalization**. Phase 4 is critical — source-quality audit, counterargument search, and logic/coherence review. *"Survive Phase 4 → ready to build. Fail it → you just saved months."*

## The Assignment (due "next week" per the deck)

1. Pick one **problem** space (not a solution); run the 5-phase workflow.
2. Produce a complete BrainLift: DOK 1 (≥10 facts, 5+ sources) · DOK 2 (organized tree) · DOK 3 (≥5 insights) · DOK 4 (≥2 Spiky POVs that survive your own counterargument search).
3. Define a distinct **User Persona** (who uses it) and **ICP** (who pays) — they must be different people.

## Relevance to This Project

This is the disciplined research practice behind the [[research]] work already done on evolutionary robotics / multi-LLM design — the primary-source gathering and red-teaming map directly onto Phases 2 and 4.
