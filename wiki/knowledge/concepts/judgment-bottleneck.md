# The Judgment Bottleneck

**Core thesis of the Cohort 5 masterclass**: AI has commoditized software *execution*, so the binding constraint on value creation has moved from "how do we build it?" to "what should we build, why, and does it matter?"

## The Argument

For ~20 years the hardest part of software was *how* to build it. The masterclass cites a roughly **99.8% drop in engineer-hours per feature** versus a 2020 baseline ("The Cliff," sourced to a16z / Epoch / internal data). With execution near-free:

- **Execution is commoditized** — writing code, debugging syntax, wiring CRUD is "the easy part."
- **Judgment is premium** — "one right decision" is where leverage now lives.

The slogan: **JUDGMENT > OUTPUT**. Output (lines of code, PRs, story points) counts for nothing unless behavior changed in production.

## Why It Matters Here

This reframes what makes a capstone valuable. It does **not** contradict [[gauntlet-capstone-brief]] (which rewards technical ambition) — rather, it says the ambition should target a problem worth solving. A technically impressive build aimed at the wrong problem is the failure mode this concept warns against.
