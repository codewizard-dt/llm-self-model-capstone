# Persona vs. ICP

A distinction stressed in the Cohort 5 masterclass: **the person who *uses* a product and the person who *pays* for it are usually not the same** — and conflating them produces "a beautiful product nobody buys."

## User Persona — Who Uses It

Name · age · role · context. Captures **goals** (what they want to accomplish) and **concerns** (what slows them down). Drives → UX decisions, feature design, onboarding, interaction patterns.

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) — Who Pays For It

Demographics · psychographics · income/budget authority · segment · journey (Awareness › Consideration › Purchase › Retention). Drives → pricing, messaging, channel strategy, go-to-market.

## Why It Matters

Every feature must trace back to a **validated pain point** (with interview evidence). Persona drives the build; ICP drives the business model. The [[brainlift]] assignment explicitly requires defining both and explaining why they differ.
