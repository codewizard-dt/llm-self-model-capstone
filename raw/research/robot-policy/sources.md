## Excerpts

### S1 — What is policy in reinforcement learning? (GeeksforGeeks)
https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/machine-learning/what-is-policy-in-reinforcement-learning/
> "A policy is simply the strategy or rulebook that the agent follows to decide what action to take in any given situation."
> "For example if a robot is learning to walk then policy will guide it on how to move its legs depending on their current position."

### S2 — Spinning Up: Key Concepts in RL (OpenAI)
https://spinningup.openai.com/en/latest/spinningup/rl_intro.html
> "Actions come from an agent according to its policy."
> "When the agent is able to observe the complete state of the environment, we say that the environment is fully observed."

### S3 — Learning for a Robot: Deep RL, Imitation Learning, Transfer Learning (PMC/NCBI)
https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7916895/
> "The essence of BC is direct policy learning, which enables the distribution of state-action trajectory generated by the agent to match the given teaching trajectory. The traditional method of behavioral cloning is that the robotic arm learns the trajectory of the movement by manual guidance or teaching box."

### S4 — Imitation Learning (MIT Underactuated Robotics)
https://underactuated.mit.edu/imitation.html
> "Behavior cloning attempts to learn a policy directly from the data using supervised learning. Inverse RL (aka inverse optimal control) attempts to learn a cost function from the data, and then uses potentially more traditional optimal control approaches to synthesize a policy for this cost function."
> "Levine16 introduced the concept of a 'visuomotor policy', and produced fairly stunning videos of robots performing complicated tasks. The name 'visuomotor' is chosen to emphasize that the policy is trained to predict actions directly from RGB camera images."

### S7 — Vision Language Action Models & Policies for Robots (LearnOpenCV)
https://learnopencv.com/vision-language-action-models-lerobot-policy/
> "Traditionally, individual policies or complex heuristics are created for each low level tasks and for specific robotic hardware configuration. But real world data is complex and training plethora of these policies are costly, time consuming and requires lot of data."
> "Though LLM performs great for text-based tasks, but they lack or limited by their understanding about physical constraints of environments in which robots operate upon."

### S8 — Generative AI in Industrial Robotics (EVS International, 2026)
https://www.evsint.com/generative-ai-industrial-robotics-foundation-models-code-generation-2026/
> "Microsoft Research's Robotics Copilot project, published in 2024, demonstrated an architecture where natural-language skill descriptions are converted to structured robot policy code through an LLM intermediary layer."
> "Figure AI's Helix (2024–25)... takes a two-model approach: a high-level VLM handles scene understanding while a low-level sensorimotor model executes at control frequency, separating the language-reasoning loop from the real-time control loop."
