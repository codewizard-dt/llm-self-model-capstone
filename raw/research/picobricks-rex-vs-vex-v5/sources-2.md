## Excerpts

### S2 — Tamiya USA: Microcomputer Robot (Crawler Type)
https://www.tamiyausa.com/shop/educational-construction/microcomputer-robot/
> "Assembly kit: It creates a crawler robot that runs using a BBC micro:bit microcomputer, ultrasonic sensor and motor driver circuit."
> "When the ultrasonic sensor identifies an obstacle ahead, it transmits that information to the BBC micro:bit, which uses a pre-installed program to give the motors commands to avoid the obstacle."
> "Different programs are available on the Tamiya website allowing the programming of a separately sold BBC micro:bit as a remote controller."

### S3 — TamiyaBlog: Official product and programming video
https://tamiyablog.com/2019/06/official-product-and-programming-video-of-tamiya-71201-microcomputer-robot-crawler-type/
> "It is equipped with a twin motor gear box that contains two low-rotation 130 motors that are less prone to noise. Because the gearbox is transparent, the mechanical mood is also plentiful. The crawler adopts the connection type which can be connected only by fitting in soft plastic."

### S5 — GigaParts: Thames and Kosmos 620401 Robotics Workshop with MicroBit
https://www.gigaparts.com/thames-and-kosmos-620401-robotics-workshop-with-microbit.html
> "Servo Motor: Make precise movements! The precision motor allows your robot to make accurate and powerful movements with complete motor control! One servo motor included."
> "The ultrasonic sensor works like a sonar system and helps your robots gauge distances and determine where objects are. Two ultrasonic sensors included."
> "The microbit: Your robots' brain, eyes, ears, voice, and more! This pocket-sized circuit board hosts your programmed code and is housed in a sleek yet durable smart controller. Upload code to the micro:bit with the included USB cord or wirelessly via Bluetooth low-energy connection."

### S6 — STEMfinity: Robotics Workshop with Micro:Bit

> "Build and program robots with the Robotics Workshop and Micro kit! Perfect for Grades 3-12+, this kit includes 248 reusable pieces and 20 experiments to teach coding and robotics. Utilize MakeCode, Python, or JavaScript to bring your creations to life."
