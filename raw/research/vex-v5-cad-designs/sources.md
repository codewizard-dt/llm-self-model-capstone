### S11 — VEX Wheels Catalog
https://www.vexrobotics.com/wheels.html
> "3.25\" Omni-Directional Wheels are compatible with All VEX V5 shafts (0.25\" and 0.125\" square bars). 2\" Mecanum Wheels are compatible with High Strength (0.25\") drive shafts, when also using VersaHex Adapters."

### S12 — VEX Drivetrain KB
https://kb.vex.com/hc/en-us/articles/360035952771-Creating-a-V5-Drivetrain
> "The H Drive uses three or five motors with four Omni-Directional Wheels and a fifth Omni-Directional Wheel set perpendicular between the other wheels of the drivetrain."
> "The Track Drive is another variation of the Standard Drivetrain [that] uses the Tank Tread Kit instead of wheels. It can easily go over obstacles."

### S13 — VEX IQ 3D Print Specification (applies to V5/EDR)
https://kb.vex.com/hc/en-us/articles/360044560952-Creating-Your-Own-Parts-for-VEX-IQ
> "All VEX IQ parts use the same 12.70 mm (1/2 in) spacing and 3.18 mm (1/8 in) square shaft as VEX EDR. We suggest that all 3D printed VEX IQ compatible parts should be designed with only holes, to be mounted using existing VEX IQ connector pins, standoffs, and/or corner connectors."

### S14 — VEX C-Channel Product Page
https://www.vexrobotics.com/channel.html
> "VEX C-Channel has holes on 0.500\" increments. Made from cold rolled steel, or 5052-H32 Aluminum. Each channel is segmented into cuttable 2.5\" pieces."
