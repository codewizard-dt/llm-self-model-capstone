## Excerpts

### S2 — Rubber Band Size Chart: Dimensions in Inches and Millimeters
https://bulkrubberbands.com/blogs/the-stretch-report/rubber-band-size-chart-dimensions-in-inches-and-millimeters
> "A size #32 rubber band measures 3 inches long by 1/8 inch wide, which is 76 mm by 3.2 mm. It is one of the most popular general-purpose sizes. A size #64 rubber band measures 3-1/2 inches long by 1/4 inch wide, or 89 mm by 6.4 mm."

### S3 — Rubber Band Size Calculator (Alliance Rubber Company)
https://www.rubberband.com/rubber-band-size-calculator/
> "Generally, rubber bands are numbered from smallest to largest, with width first. Thus, rubber bands numbered 8–19 are all 1⁄16 inch wide, with lengths going from 7⁄8 inch to 3-1⁄2 inches. Rubber band numbers 30–35 are for widths of 1⁄8 inch, going again from shorter to longer."

### S4 — VEX V5 Robotics Competition Robot Inspection Details (RECF Library)
https://kb.roboticseducation.org/hc/en-us/articles/13230666960407
> "#32 is 3"x1/8" when not stretched under load. #64 is 3-½"x1/4". 117B is approximately 7"in diameter. Rubber bands may come in different colors, and all colors are legal."

### S5 — VEX Robotics rubber-bands.html (via search snippet)
https://www.vexrobotics.com/rubber-bands.html
> "VEX Rubber Bands have dozens of uses on a robot. Their spring-like properties make them perfect for latches, triggers, and return mechanisms. EPDM Rubber Bands and Synthetic Rubber Bands work better for high elongation uses, while Silicone Rubber Bands have a higher coefficient of friction against many types of plastic, enabling your intake mechanisms to grip objects easier."

### S6 — 10 Epic VEX Robot Builds (Robot Instructions)
https://robotinstructions.com/vex-robot-builds/
> "Rubber bands #32—the goldilocks size for linear tensioning … Rubber-band 'passive assistance' is legal in VRC: a well-tuned linear band can reduce motor load by 30%, letting you run 1-motor lifts that out-cycle 2-motor bots."

### S7 — Smart Autonomous Robot Design for VEX U Challenge "In the Zone" (LACCEI 2018)
https://www.laccei.org/LACCEI2018-Lima/student_Papers/SP92.pdf
> "intake #64 rubber bands were added to the intake… the addition of rubber bands increases the rate at which the lift expands and contracts."
